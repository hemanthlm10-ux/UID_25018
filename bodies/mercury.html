// Overview

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun, completing one orbit every 88 Earth days. Despite its proximity to the Sun, it is not the hottest planet — its lack of atmosphere means it cannot trap heat. Daytime temperatures soar to 430°C while nights plunge to −180°C, the most extreme temperature swing of any planet.
